Transportation Services Contract

1. Parties and Purpose

This Contract is entered into on October 1, 2024, between the Michigan Department of Health and Human Services (hereinafter referred to as the "Agency"), located in Lansing, Michigan, and Great Lakes Medical Transport (hereinafter referred to as the "Provider"), located in Detroit, Michigan. The purpose of this Contract is to provide non-emergency medical transportation services to eligible individuals in Wayne County, Oakland County, and Macomb County.

2. Definitions

To be defined in this section.

3. Term and Renewal

This Contract shall be effective for a period of two (2) years from the date of execution. The Agency reserves the right to renew this Contract for up to three (3) additional one-year periods, subject to satisfactory performance by the Provider and the availability of funds.

4. Scope of Services

The Provider shall provide non-emergency medical transportation services to eligible individuals residing in Wayne County, Oakland County, and Macomb County. Services shall include, but not be limited to, transportation to and from medical appointments, dialysis treatments, and other approved destinations.

5. Provider Responsibilities

The Provider shall maintain a fleet of at least 65 vehicles and employ a minimum of 155 drivers to provide transportation services. The Provider shall operate 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, to ensure the availability of services. The Provider shall comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws, regulations, and guidelines related to the provision of non-emergency medical transportation services.

6. Agency Responsibilities

The Agency shall be responsible for determining eligibility criteria for individuals to receive transportation services under this Contract. The Agency shall provide the Provider with a list of eligible individuals and their approved destinations. The Agency shall monitor the Provider's performance and ensure compliance with the terms of this Contract.

7. Compensation

To be defined in this section.

8. Compliance and Reporting

The Provider shall maintain accurate records and submit regular reports to the Agency regarding the services provided under this Contract. The Provider shall cooperate with the Agency in any audits or reviews conducted to ensure compliance with the terms of this Contract.

9. Insurance and Liability

The Provider shall maintain adequate insurance coverage, including but not limited to general liability, automobile liability, and workers' compensation insurance, as required by the Agency. The Provider shall indemnify and hold harmless the Agency from any claims, losses, or damages arising from the Provider's performance under this Contract.

10. Termination

This Contract may be terminated by either party upon written notice, as specified in the Contract. The Agency reserves the right to terminate the Contract immediately in the event of a material breach by the Provider.
