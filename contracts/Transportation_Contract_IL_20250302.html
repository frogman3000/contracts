Transportation Services Contract

Contract Date: July 15, 2024

Agency: Illinois Department of Healthcare and Family Services

Agency Location: Springfield, Illinois

Provider: Midwest Healthcare Transit

Provider Location: Chicago, Illinois

1. Parties and Purpose

This Contract is entered into between the Illinois Department of Healthcare and Family Services (the "Agency") and Midwest Healthcare Transit (the "Provider") for the purpose of providing non-emergency medical transportation services to eligible individuals in the State of Illinois.

2. Definitions

For the purposes of this Contract, the following terms shall have the meanings set forth below:

  • "Eligible Individual" means a person who is eligible to receive non-emergency medical transportation services under the Agency's programs.

  • "Service Area" means the geographic area within which the Provider shall provide transportation services, which includes Cook County, DuPage County, and Lake County.

3. Term and Renewal

This Contract shall have an initial term of two (2) years, commencing on July 15, 2024, and ending on July 14, 2026. The Agency shall have the option to renew this Contract for two (2) additional one-year periods, upon written notice to the Provider at least sixty (60) days prior to the expiration of the then-current term.

4. Scope of Services

The Provider shall provide non-emergency medical transportation services to Eligible Individuals within the Service Area. Services shall include, but not be limited to, transportation to and from medical appointments, hospitals, and other healthcare facilities.

5. Provider Responsibilities

The Provider shall:

  • Maintain a fleet of at least 60 vehicles, including vehicles equipped to transport individuals with disabilities.

  • Operate transportation services 24 hours per day, 7 days per week.

  • Employ a minimum of 145 qualified and properly licensed drivers.

  • Ensure that all vehicles are properly licensed, insured, and maintained in good working condition.

  • Comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws, regulations, and ordinances.

6. Agency Responsibilities

The Agency shall:

  • Provide the Provider with a list of Eligible Individuals and their transportation needs.

  • Establish and communicate policies and procedures for the provision of transportation services.

  • Monitor the Provider's performance and compliance with the terms of this Contract.

7. Compensation

The Agency shall compensate the Provider for transportation services rendered in accordance with the rates and payment schedule set forth in Exhibit A, attached hereto and incorporated herein by reference.

8. Compliance and Reporting

The Provider shall maintain accurate and complete records of all transportation services provided under this Contract and shall submit reports to the Agency as required. The Provider shall also comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and Agency policies related to the provision of non-emergency medical transportation services.

9. Insurance and Liability

The Provider shall maintain adequate insurance coverage, including but not limited to commercial general liability, automobile liability, and workers' compensation insurance, as specified in Exhibit B, attached hereto and incorporated herein by reference. The Provider shall indemnify and hold harmless the Agency from any and all claims, losses, damages, and expenses arising out of or resulting from the Provider's performance under this Contract.

10. Termination

This Contract may be terminated by either party, with or without cause, upon ninety (90) days' written notice to the other party. The Agency may also terminate this Contract immediately for cause, including but not limited to the Provider's failure to comply with the terms of this Contract or applicable laws and regulations.

11. General Provisions

This Contract shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Illinois. Any amendments or modifications to this Contract must be in writing and signed by authorized representatives of both parties. This Contract constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior agreements, whether written or oral.
